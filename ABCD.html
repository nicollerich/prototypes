Arthur Matson

Designer & Illustrator of the Mid-20th Century

SEPTEMBER 25, 2015

Arthur Matson has led a very interesting, Mad Men, life. Born in 1928, Arthur has spent his whole life revolved around art and design. He started learning how to draw and paint at Worcester Art Museum High School Saturday Art Classes, 1945-1946, to then enroll at Mass.College of Art in 1946. Post-Korean War he started his profession as a designer and illustrator where he worked, and eventually took over, at a design studio called Fannel Studio in Boston. Later he left that company to freelance. 

Arthur has designed for many companies like American Airlines, to nation-wide businesses, to small local mom and pop shops. He's been retired since 1981 and is now an occasional watercolorist. Arthur lives with his wife, Nancy, in Uxbridge, MA and is a proud father of three, grandfather of seven, and great-grandfather of three.

Sarah Sampsel

Director of Digital Strategy and Design, The Washington Post

SEPTEMBER 18, 2015

Since 2007, Sarah has split her time between D.C. and N.Y.C., designing interactive graphics, blockbuster investigations and new digital products like The Washington Post for iPad. In 2011, she became design director, growing the team and pushing to develop tools for creation of multimedia-rich story presentations, and a fully-responsive mobile-first article page.

About a year and a half ago, she moved to New York to start a small product design group focused on a site-wide rethink, coordinating all design, development, product and advertising teams involved.
